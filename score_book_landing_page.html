Right now, your career lives in a filing cabinet you don't control.

Tournament apps handle registration and squadding well. They were never built to be a place you'd want to open between events — no trend lines, no personal record book, no way to see how you've done against the people you actually shoot with.

A serious sport deserves a serious record.

Runners have Strava. Cyclists have it too. Skeet shooters have a printed scoresheet that gets thrown away at the end of the day. This is the place your classification progress, your class wins, and your head-to-head history actually live — permanently, and portably.

What it does

Built around the eight stations you already know, not a generic stat dashboard.

01 — Your record

Every round, permanently

Connect your NSSA account once. Every event you've shot, every gauge, every class — pulled in and kept, not re-entered by hand.

02 — Your trajectory

Classification over time

Watch your class move as your season does. See the shape of your progress, not just this week's number.

03 — Your rivals

Head-to-head history

Pick anyone you've squadded against. See every time your paths have crossed, and who came out ahead.

04 — Your club

Leaderboards worth looking at

A live, shareable event page for your club — built for free, no cost or lock-in, better than a printed scoreboard.

Starting small, on purpose.

This is being built and piloted first inside the Massachusetts and New England skeet circuit — Minute Man, Massapoag, Old Colony, Ludlow, and the rest of the clubs already shooting together every season.

If that's your circuit too, you'll be among the first people to actually use it.
