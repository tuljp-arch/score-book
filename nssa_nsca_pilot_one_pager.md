# A shooter profile and analytics layer for competitive clay sports
### One-page overview for NSSA/NSCA

**What this is**

A shooter-facing app that gives every competitor a permanent, portable profile of their competitive history — scores, classification progress, personal bests, and head-to-head history with other shooters — across skeet, trap, and sporting clays. Think of it as the layer that sits *on top of* tournament registration and scoring, not a replacement for it.

**What this is not**

Not a registration or scoring platform. Score Chaser and Winscore already do that job well for clubs and tournament directors, and we have no interest in competing there. We pull results in after they're scored; we don't run the shoot.

**Why it's worth doing**

Today, a shooter's competitive record lives scattered across tournament results pages, club records, and personal memory. There's no single place a shooter (or a club, or a sponsor) can see a career: classification progress over time, performance trends, or how they've done against people they've squadded against. That's a retention and engagement gap the sport is currently leaving on the table — and it's the same gap Strava filled for running and cycling, on top of infrastructure (Garmin, race timing systems) it never tried to replace.

**What it does for the sport**

- Gives clubs a better, free leaderboard and event page than a printed scoreboard — no cost, no lock-in
- Gives shooters a reason to engage with the sport between events, which is good for participation and renewal
- Gives NSSA/NSCA a clearer, quantified picture of engagement across the membership — something that strengthens the case to sponsors and manufacturers, which benefits the whole ecosystem including the associations themselves

**What we're asking for**

Not exclusivity, and not access to anything sensitive. Just an early, informal conversation about:
1. Whether a lightweight data-sharing arrangement (e.g. results export access, or a future API) is something NSSA/NSCA would be open to as this develops
2. Any concerns from your side we should be designing around from day one, rather than retrofitting later

**Pilot plan**

We're piloting this at our own club-level and state-level events this season (Massachusetts States, 8/23) before bringing it to anyone else. Happy to share what we learn — good or bad — as a gesture of good faith before any broader conversation.
